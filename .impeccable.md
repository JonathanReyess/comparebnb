# CompareBnB — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
The **Designated Planner** of a friend group or extended family — the person who volunteers (or gets voluntold) to organize the group trip. They have 15 tabs open, a chaotic WhatsApp thread, and a spreadsheet that's getting out of hand. They love organization but are overwhelmed by the decision surface.

**Job to be done:** Transform scattered listing links and group opinions into a single, shareable, defensible decision — fast.

**Emotional arc:** Arrive feeling overwhelmed → leave feeling relieved and confident.

They appreciate polish because they're spending serious money. They don't need to be sold — they need to be helped.

### Brand Personality
**Decisive · Breezy · Polished**

- **Decisive:** The UI should actively guide toward a conclusion. Highlight winners, surface "best for X" signals, and never leave the user stranded in ambiguity.
- **Breezy:** This is vacation planning, not a tax audit. Interactions should feel light, fast, and airy. Copy should be warm and human, never corporate.
- **Polished:** Premium feel appropriate for high-spend decisions. Every spacing choice, shadow, and transition should feel intentional.

### Aesthetic Direction
**References:**
- **Airbnb** — warmth, photography-led, hospitality feel. Aspirational without being cold.
- **Linear** — precision and intentionality in utility UI. Every pixel earns its place. The comparison table and wizard steps should channel this.
- **Family (formerly Honest)** — soft, beautiful UI components with smooth, meaningful transitions.

**Anti-references:**
- **Booking.com** — no fake urgency, no "5 people are looking at this right now", no aggressive red text, no dark patterns.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards** — no heavy sidebars, no dense data tables that look like a CRM or Google Analytics report.

**Visual language:** Light mode, white and gray-50 surfaces, brand blue (#0d69ea) as the action color, emerald for winner/positive states. Full-screen video hero with frosted glass overlays. Rounded corners (2xl–3xl), generous spacing, soft shadows. Think travel magazine meets pro tool.

### Design Principles

1. **Relief over information density.** Every screen should reduce cognitive load, not add to it. Prefer one clear call-to-action over multiple options. Use whitespace as a feature, not a gap to fill.

2. **Winners, not just data.** The tool's job is to help people decide. Always highlight the best value, the top pick, or the winner — don't make users interpret raw data themselves.

3. **Meaningful motion, never decorative.** Transitions should tell the user where they are in the flow (wizard sliding left-to-right signals progress). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` at all times. Motion reinforces understanding, not aesthetics.

4. **Premium trust signals.** Since users are making high-spend decisions, the UI must feel trustworthy and considered. No clutter, no dark patterns, no filler copy. Precision in every label, shadow, and interaction state.

5. **Accessibility without compromise.** Target WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Brand blue (#0d69ea) must meet 4.5:1 contrast on white for small text — validate and adjust as needed. All interactive states must be keyboard-navigable with visible focus rings.
